Roof Sheeting for Steel Structures

Advantages of Steel Sheets

There is a wide range of sheeting options available for steel structures. When choosing sheets for your structure there are many facts to consider for the best option.

1

Quick to fit.

The longer it takes to complete your roofing, the more it will cost. Steel roofing sheets are quick to fit. Up to 1200 sqm of roof sheeting can be completed in a day, by a competent roof sheeting team.

2

Unique look – which is immensely popular.

3

Wide range of colours.

4

Continuous sheet – less leaks.

Cost, durability & appearance are the most important facts influencing your choice.

There are different types of material available: Galvanised sheeting, Colourbond, Zincal, Chromadeck; concealed fix roof sheets, fibre cement sheeting.

The profiles of the sheet (also known as pitch & depth) are the appearance of the sheets and are also the effective coverage of the width of the sheets : IBR which is 0.686mm coverage; Widespan which is 0.762mm and Corrugated which is also 0.762mm.

Types of sheets:

There are a number of different types of roofing sheets

  • Galved Sheets - these are sheets which are cold rolled out of a flat coil & is produced on a continuous zinc coating line. Galved sheets come in a range of thickness. The prefix “Z” in the coating indicates a pure zinc coating gram per m2. The heavier the coating the longer the corrosion protection. Galved sheets are used in areas of high ammonia levels such as chicken sheds, piggeries, and fertilizer sheds 
  • Chromadeck Sheeting - Is a galvanised based sheet with a painted top colour primer coat. The paint is applied under controlled conditions on a continuous paint line which makes it corrosion resistance. This reduces maintenance costs as it does not require repainting even after many years of being installed. The paint coating is cured at elevated temperatures & makes it unique with its improved gloss & colour retention. Its heat reflective colour makes it energy efficient. Chromadeck offers versatility for roofing & cladding applications such as churches, shopping malls, warehouses, airports & residential buildings. It is suitable in and near coastal areas where there is a demanding environment in Africa. Chromadeck is also used in mildly chemical polluted areas. 
  • Zincal Sheets - These are sheets which comprises of 55% Aluminium, 43.5% Zinc and 1.5% Silicon. Zincal sheets are a better option in Coastal areas but not remarkably close to the sea…like 200m from the sea. They are also a better option for industrial & rural sites due to the zinc and aluminium coating. This offers a double barrier protection against corrosion. Zincal sheeting has a corrosion resistance up to 4x the life of a galvanised sheet with a similar coating thickness.
  • Colourbond & Colour plus - is a zincal/aluminium based sheet with a topcoat colour primer. It has a higher corrosion resistance due to the zincal steel coat. There is a wide range of colours. The paint system used in the manufacturing of Colourbond sheets has a high degree of resin stability with its multi layers of coating. A polyester primer is also baked onto the surface, thereafter a topcoat of exterior grade paint is baked onto the topside. This is to prevent chipping, peeling & cracking. This sheeting is excellent to use in coastal areas, severe industrial environments & does not fade in colour.
  • Concealed Fix Roof sheeting - This is a roof sheeting system that is used in wider span buildings. There are virtually no leaks due to its hidden fasteners (concealed fixing) which also enhances the aesthetic appeal. There are many advantages of using this type of sheeting. The unrestricted thermal movement allows for expansion & contraction of sheeting without subjecting to fasteners fatigue & loosening. Double Capillary action break: The design of the interlocking mechanism of adjoining sheets ensures the prevention of rainwater entering. Security: As fasteners are concealed, the roof sheets cannot be easily removed from the outside. Profiling & lengths: The sheets can be rolled on site to reduce transport cost & damage due to the sheet’s long length. Reusable: This is an advantage if the building is to be demolished then the sheets can be reused as there will be no holes or damage. The concealed fix sheets are available in Galved, Chromadeck, Zincal, Colo plus, polycarbonate. 
  • Fibre Cement sheeting - These are sheets which are manufactured using a mixture of cellulose fibre, cement, silica & water. They are beneficial as they are made of safe renewable fibres, considerable tensile strength with enhanced dynamic load bearing properties. Fibre cement sheets have excellent thermal properties and are water & wind resistance. They are also having a high fire resistance, resistant to fungus, rodent & acid. Fibre cement sheets satisfy the needs of the commercial, industrial sectors. Profile is called big 6 but is not popular in residential sectors because of its look.
